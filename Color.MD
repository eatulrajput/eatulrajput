# Building / Making UI Color Palettes

There are three categories of colors:
1. Brand colors
2. Supporting colors
3. Neutrals

Brand Colors
 Brand colors comes from business. 
 It is used mostly in buttons, Links, Navigation and Icons

Supporting Colors
 Supporting colors are used throughout the interface, and key important where you need to draw attention or communicate something to the user using color.
 These are often less than brand colors.
 You will see them in error messages, confirmations, or information dialogs.

Neutrals
 Most of the app will be made up of colors from the neutrals category.
 Most of theme will gray: Text, background, border colors, and secondary buttons.

Steps to build color palettes:
1. Brand Color, Find a starting point
    Pick one color, and expand from it in a systematic way
    Choose a good middle color, on that color your lighter and darker shades will be based on.
    There is scientific way based for it.
    
    Rule of Thumb:
    - Color which work well with button background
    - While creating color palette, use HSB format. H is hue for color(1-365), S stands for saturation, which tell how intense the color is (0-100),B stands for brightness, which means how much black or white you are adding to the color(0-100).

2. Supporting colors
    These colors will be used in few specific places when you need to draw attention or communicate something to the user.
    - Green for success or other positive confirmations.
    - Orange or Yellow for warnings
    - Red for dangerous situation, like form error
    - Blue for neutral information or messages
    Supporting colors need to feel like they are going with the brand color.
    If your brand color is saturated, and supporting colors are really desaturated, the UI might feel like odd or disconnected.
    - For this pay attention to the saturation and brightness stay close to your base color
    - They you can adjust the hue, however you want.

3. Create bunch of shades
    Colors will be used in all kinds of different places throughout the UI
    So, we probably want 5-10 shades.
    Take a 9 blocks, label them to 100 to 900
    Put the base color in the middel spot of 500
    Shades must be an arc.
    Lightest shade must have S[5-10], B[90-100]
    Darkest shade must have S[90-100], B[5-10]

    Where are they going to be used, the darkest shaded will be used for text color. Lightest shade will be used for the tinted background.

---

How to apply a color palette to your design?

The rule: 60-30-10
Primary color: 60 %
Secondary color: 30 %
Accent Color: 10 %

Start basing with background color.

Color is about context. Each color must have a context.

---

How to choose colors?
1. Choose a dominant color.
2. Add two more colors, you will have a palette of 3 colors.

Dominant color, complementary color, Accent

Rule borrowed from interior design: 60/30/10 rule
